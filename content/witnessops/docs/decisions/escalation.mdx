# Do I Need to Escalate?

Use this page to decide when work must stop locally and move to a higher authority.

Escalation is not failure. It is the control that prevents unauthorized certainty.

## 1. Problem this page solves

Governed work fails when escalation is implicit, delayed, or personality-based.

This page defines when risk or uncertainty has moved beyond local decision authority.

## 2. What you should understand after reading

After this page, you should understand:

- which escalation triggers are in scope
- which risk or uncertainty patterns require escalation
- what to do before and after escalation
- when escalation is required even if execution still seems possible

## 3. Mechanism-first escalation path

1. **Identify the active task or decision.** Clarify the current objective and the planned action.
2. **Identify escalation signals.** Detect scope ambiguity, weak evidence, elevated risk, failed checks, or authority gaps.
3. **Check local resolution authority.** Decide whether you are allowed to resolve the issue within current boundaries.
4. **If local resolution is not allowed, stop or hold.** Do not continue high-risk or ambiguous execution.
5. **Escalate to the correct authority.** Route the case to the approver or response lead with decision context.
6. **Preserve evidence and rationale.** Make sure the escalation package is reviewable and actionable.

Escalation is required when the system cannot justify continued action within the current authority, evidence, or risk limits.

## 4. Observed vs inferred

| Layer | What is observed | What is inferred |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Observed | scope conflicts, missing approvals, contradictory artifacts, failed checks, explicit risk signals | none beyond recorded signals |
| Inferred | judgment that uncertainty or risk is too high to continue safely | depends on operator interpretation and context pressure |

## 5. Trust assumptions

Escalation quality depends on assumptions that can fail:

- operators can underestimate risk
- evidence can look cleaner than the underlying reality
- time pressure can distort judgment
- escalation outcome depends on correct routing and preserved context

## 6. Explicit decision outcomes

| Outcome | Condition | Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Continue locally | risk or uncertainty remains within authority and evidence limits | proceed under governed controls |
| Stop and escalate | authority, evidence, or risk boundary exceeded | halt local progression and escalate |
| Preserve and hold | escalation is needed but immediate execution is unsafe or unclear | preserve artifacts and wait for decision |
| Redirect to lab or non-production path | normal governed path is not applicable but a controlled exception is possible | use documented exception controls |
| Deny action pending clarification | requested action cannot be justified with current inputs | do not execute until clarified or approved |
